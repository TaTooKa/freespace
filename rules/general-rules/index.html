RULES REFERENCE

GAME MODES

This game can be played in SOLO, COOP or GUIDED mode.

The player(s) say what their character(s) do and describe their actions in the fiction. When a PROMPT is triggered, they must follow the directives on it.

In GUIDED mode, the GUIDE decides what happens with everything other than the player characters (NPCs, the environment, etc), and adjudicates the consequences of the player characters actions (especially when there is a FAILURE in their tests).

In SOLO or COOP mode, all players adjudicate the outcomes of tests and the actions and reactions of NPCs and the environment (either as a discussion or by taking turns).

As a rule of thumb, each player has final say on the descriptions of actions and outcomes of their own character, but they must follow the directives on each PROMPT.


DICE TESTS

When required to test a STAT (BRAIN, CHROME, EDGE, FLASH or SHADE), roll d100 against that value. If equal or under, its a SUCCESS. If over that value, its a FAILURE.

A match of units and tens is a CRITICAL (SUCCESS or FAILURE), meaning that the result is greatly exacerbated, for good or ill. On top of that, a 01 is always a CRITICAL SUCCESS and a 100 is always a CRITICAL FAILURE.

Note that some tests are made against a challenge value instead of a STAT (like in the case of FULFILL A CHALLENGE or RETIRE).


BOOSTS AND GLITCHES

A BOOST is an advantage or edge you get for a test. A GLITCH is the opposite; a disadvantage or a bad condition that lowers your chances for a test.

Sometimes youll be given one or more BOOSTS or GLITCHES for a test. When this happens:

  • tally all BOOSTS and GLITCHES and cancel each other 1:1 (If you had the same amount of BOOSTS and GLITCHES, they cancel each other for a regular d100 roll).
  • Then, if you have any BOOSTS or GLITCHES remaining, roll an extra d% (the tens die) for each BOOST or GLITCH, and keep the best one if you had BOOSTS, or the worst one if you had GLITCHES.
  • To keep the best one or the worst one means that you get to pick the lowest result OR the one that would guarantee a CRITICAL SUCCESS in the case of a BOOSTS; and you keep the highest result OR the one that would net you a CRITICAL FAILURE in the case of a GLITCH.

Note that you still always roll one die for the units (important to compare in case of CRITICALS, since the odds increase for each d% added).


CHARACTER STATE

Your character can be in one of two states:

IN CONTROL or IN A BAD SPOT.

This represents an overall state that is self-explanatory: You either have command over a situation or the general narrative (you have the upper hand), or you are at a disadvantageous position or stumbling, and things can go wrong at any moment.

This state has diegetic significance that changes your fictional positioning (envision your actions and descriptions accordingly), but it also has mechanical implications: Some of the PROMPTS will have different outcomes whether you are IN CONTROL or IN A BAD SPOT, and they can also alter your state depending on the result of your dice.


CHALLENGES

Challenges have a progress value that goes from 1 to 99.

They also have a rank, that determines how long they take (how much progress you make when advancing towards your objective):

  • SHORT: Progress by increments of +30% (it takes 4 progress to maximize chances). It nets 2 XP when fulfilled.
  • MEDIUM: Progress by increments of +20% (it takes 5 progress to maximize chances). It nets 5 XP when fulfilled.
  • LONG: Progress by increments of +10% (it takes 10 progress to maximize chances). It nets 10 XP when fulfilled.
  • EXTREME: Progress by increments of +5% (it takes 20 progress to maximize chances). It nets 20 XP when fulfilled.
  • EPIC: Progress by increments of +2% (it takes 50 progress to maximize chances). It nets 50 XP when fulfilled.

Whenever there is a need for an involved scene (a chase scene, an action sequence, a combat encounter, a hacking duel, etc), you should ACCEPT A CHALLENGE (if the obstacle or narrative beat can be resolved with a single action, then you dont need a challenge).

  • When a challenge begins, its value starts at 01%.
  • When you are instructed to MAKE PROGRESS, you add the corresponding increment to the Challenge value. It cannot go over 99%.
  • When you want to FULFILL A CHALLENGE, you have to roll a d100. If the result is lower or equal than the Challenge value, you succeed. If its higher, you fail.
  • A result of 100 is always a failure; a result of 01 is always a success.

CHARACTER CREATION

  • Think of a concept for your character. Then give them a name (or use an oracle).
  • Set their XP current/total to 0/0.
  • Set their status to IN CONTROL.
  • Distribute the following values however you like among your STATS: 60, 50, 40, 30, 20.
  • Set your GRIT, PSYCHE and GEAR to 5.
  • Choose your ANGLE from the options provided. Your ANGLE is the objective you want to accomplish before you RETIRE. You can flesh it out or give it more details in the NOTES section, if you want.
  • Choose 3 TRAITS from the SKILLS AND BACKGROUND, GEAR AND CYBERWARE and CONTACTS sections.

If you are playing SOLO (or GUIDED with a single PC) consider awarding that lone character a starting 50XP bonus to IMPROVE YOURSELF and increase some of their STATS or buy some extra TRAITS (otherwise the high probability of failures will make your first missions very hard).


BEGINNING A GAME

  • All players involved should envision and/or discuss the cyberpunk setting theyll be exploring in the fiction (available tech, major factions and opposition, nature and name of THE CITY, etc).

The players should create their characters together and decide what they do and why they are together, working as a team.

  • Each character has their own objectives, defined by their ANGLE, and thats what each of them should be pursuing (they can decide to directly help each other or not). The ANGLE works as an EPIC challenge, meaning that it will take lots of attempts for each character to progress and eventually fulfill it. Once all of thats clear, envision an inciting incident, ACCEPT A CHALLENGE and get to work on it.

ENDING A GAME

  • this game can be played indefinitely (i.e. you can RETIRE a character and continue playing with a new one, but the main plot or the overarching story continues),
  • ...or it can be played with a set destination (i.e. when every character retires or dies, the game is over),
  • ...or it can also be played as a one-shot, in which a single short story plays out and then the game ends.
The decision is up to the players.
